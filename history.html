History

History

The organization, known as PRCD or Professional Remote Control Drifters, was formally founded in Manila on September 9, 2006. However, the roots of RC drifting in Manila date back to 2004 when enthusiasts were already experimenting with various drifting techniques using improvised drift tires made from 2-inch PVC and ABS pipes.

When PRCD was established, it began with 21 members, and a committee of seven took charge of managing the group's activities and operations. These dedicated committee members played crucial roles in organizing events, competitions, and other drift-related activities that contributed to the flourishing RC drifting community in the Philippines.

In the early days, enthusiasts had limited options for specialized drift equipment, but in 2007-2008, major RC manufacturers like Yokomo and Tamiya recognized the growing popularity of RC drifting and started producing purpose-built drift tires, enhancing the overall drift experience. This led to the development of dedicated drift chassis from various RC brands like HPI, Yokomo, and Tamiya, offering improved performance, stability, and adjustability.

As a result of these advancements, RC drifting gained more traction and widespread appeal, leading to the establishment of subgroups under the umbrella of Philippine RC Drifters to cater to local RC drifting communities and promote regional engagement.

Over the years, PRCD has been invited to participate in numerous prestigious car shows and events, both in the Philippines and internationally, elevating the profile of Philippine RC drifting on a global scale.

In recent times, PRCD has embraced true rear-wheel-drive (RWD) drifting, participating in RWD events and competitions, including the Thailand Drift Championship (TDC). Their dedication to improvement has paid off, with one of their members securing 2nd place in the TDC World Class competition.

Now, in 2023, PRCD, together with South Boys Drifters (SBD) and Cebu RC Drifters (CRCD), is gearing up to represent the Philippines in the TDC, which will be held in Jakarta, Indonesia for the first time. This incredible opportunity symbolizes our commitment to advancing RC drifting in the Philippines and competing at the highest level internationally.
